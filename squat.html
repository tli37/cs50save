The Squat

Proper competition depth requires the hip crease below the top of the knee

The Squat is a lift primiarily utilizing much of the lower body while also using parts of the back and abdonminal muscles.
The squat in short is done by the lifter placing the bar on their upper back and bending hips and knees reaching a bottom position and then standing up. In the IPF rulebook one of the rules states that the hip crevice must be below the upper part of the kneecap. If this is achived the lifter has performed the squat with proper depth.

Technique

There are many different aspects in achiving a squat that is optimal for each person. Bar placement, stance width and toe angle are some of the things that are going to be highly individual.
